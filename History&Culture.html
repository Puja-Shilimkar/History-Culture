Historical Timeline

  • Indus Valley Civilization (3300–1300 BCE)

    The ancient Indus Valley Civilization, known for its advanced urban planning and sophisticated culture, thrived in the Indian subcontinent.

  • Maurya Empire (322–185 BCE)

    The Maurya Empire, under the rule of Emperor Ashoka, played a significant role in the spread of Buddhism and the development of edicts promoting peace.

  • Mughal Empire (1526–1857)

    The Mughal Empire, known for its architectural wonders like the Taj Mahal, contributed to art, culture, and the blending of diverse traditions.

  • Indian Independence (1947)

    India gained independence from British rule in 1947, marking a crucial moment in its history and the beginning of a new era.

Food and Culture

The Indian culinary repertoire reflects the cultural diversity of the country. The term “Indian food” denotes a mélange of flavors from different parts of the country and showcases centuries of cultural exchange with the far corners of the world. Here, on our portal, we are making a small effort to gradually build a treasure trove of information about the countless exquisite flavors of our country. It is an ongoing venture, and over time, we aim to capture as much as possible of the incredible culinary diversity of this land.
